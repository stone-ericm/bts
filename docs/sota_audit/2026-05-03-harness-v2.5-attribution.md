### Coarse-resolution warning

The point estimates take only 3 distinct values: `0.0083`, `0.0250`, `0.0333`. At 24 seeds × 5 folds = 120 trajectory replays, these correspond exactly to **1/120, 3/120, 4/120** successful "reach 57-streak" trajectories. **The metric has integer-count resolution.** When two cells produce the same value, this means "same coarse bin," NOT necessarily "same underlying behavior" — they may differ in which folds succeeded, which seeds hit the streak, which actions the policy took, etc.

To make stronger claims about mechanism equivalence (e.g., "B and C are substitutable"), we would need to inspect:
- Per-fold contribution patterns (which fold succeeded in each cell?)
- Selected actions per (state, time)
- Per-fold parameter estimates and bin definitions

This memo does NOT do that inspection. The conclusions below are about **scalar verdict outcomes only**.

## Findings (with appropriate hedging)

### 1. A has no observed marginal effect conditional on per-fold policy

Two contrasts measure A's effect within the C=per-fold branch:
- V101 − V001 = 0.0250 − 0.0250 = 0.0pp (A turning on under scalar rho_pair)
- V111 − V011 = 0.0333 − 0.0333 = 0.0pp (A turning on under per-bin rho_pair)

Both are exactly zero at metric resolution. Note however:
- The metric resolution is 1/120 = 0.0083pp. **A could move the verdict by up to 0.0083pp without showing in this metric.**
- Cell 101 used reduced inference reps (n_bootstrap=100 vs 300, etc.) — see the caveat section below for why this likely doesn't matter for point estimates, but it's a confound to acknowledge.
- We measured A's effect inside C=per-fold ONLY. Outside that branch (cells 100 and 110) A is operationally undefined per the nested design. **We do NOT have evidence about A's effect under global policy.**

**Defensible claim**: "A is dead-zero across all measured per-fold contrasts at this metric's resolution."

**Not-defensible claim**: "Fold-local parameter estimation is methodology theater" — too strong. A's null effect on this scalar in this finite setup doesn't generalize to other datasets, MDPs, or metrics.

### 2. Both B-alone-with-global-C (cell 010) and C-alone-with-scalar-B (cell 001) reach 0.0250

V010 = V001 = 0.0250. Either change applied alone produces the same coarse scalar verdict.

**Mechanism inspection (added 2026-05-03 evening, via agent-bus pair-programming with Codex)**: read-only inspection of `fold_metadata` shows V001, V010, AND V101 all share the **identical fold-success vector** by held-out season:

| Fold | 2021 | 2022 | 2023 | 2024 | 2025 | total |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| V001, V010, V101 | 0/24 | 0/24 | 2/24 | 0/24 | 1/24 | 3/120 = 0.0250 |
| V011, V111 | 0/24 | 0/24 | 3/24 | 0/24 | 1/24 | 4/120 = 0.0333 |

V011 and V111 also share their fold vector. **The V011/V111 → 0.0333 cells gain exactly one additional success in 2023 over the V001/V010/V101 → 0.0250 cells; everything else identical.**

This moves the mechanism-equivalence hypothesis from "plausible but underdetermined" to "consistent at fold granularity." The cells succeed in the same held-out seasons with the same fold counts. Caveat: `fold_metadata` does NOT retain trajectory IDs or policy actions, so this **does NOT prove** same successful seeds/days, same policy decisions, or same error modes. It proves same seasonal-success-count pattern.

**Defensible** (after fold-level inspection): "B-with-global-C, C-with-scalar-B, and A-with-scalar-B-plus-per-fold all produce identical fold-success patterns — the verdict equality is not scalar coincidence at fold granularity."

**Still not defensible** (would need trajectory-ID inspection): "B and C are mechanism-equivalent" — successful trajectory IDs, policy actions, and seed-level outcomes were not inspected. A trajectory-level diagnostic could be added if mechanism language matters before v2.6.

### 3. Adding per-bin rho_pair to per-fold policy lifts another tick

V011 − V001 = +0.0083pp (per-bin vs scalar rho_pair, under per-fold policy). V111 − V101 also +0.0083pp. **Per-bin rho_pair on top of per-fold policy adds approximately one metric tick** (1/120 = +0.0083). This is small but consistent across the A-contrasted pairs.

**Defensible**: "Per-bin rho_pair adds one metric tick over scalar rho_pair, when policy is per-fold."

## Implications for the v2 memo

## Caveats on cell 101's reduced inference reps

Cell 101 was run with `n_bootstrap=100` (vs 300), `pa_n_bootstrap=50` (vs 300), `n_permutations=100` (vs 300), `n_final=5000` (vs 20000) due to Vultr CPU performance. Codex's earlier safety check confirmed (by reading the code paths) that these knobs control CIs and rho-shape diagnostics — NOT the `corrected_pipeline_p57` point estimate. The point is `_run_terminal_r_mc_bootstrap.point_estimate = terminal_R.mean()`, computed BEFORE bootstrapping. Reduced reps affect only CI/p-value/CE-IS metadata.

**Codex's matrix review noted (correctly) that "deterministic given seed" is necessary but not sufficient — a deterministic Monte Carlo point can still depend on n_final or other reps.** The implementation-level assertion is: `corrected_pipeline_p57` is computed as the mean of `fold_estimates`, where each `fold_estimate` = `_run_terminal_r_mc_bootstrap(traj_df, ...).point_estimate`, which is the mean of terminal-reward indicators across the 24 seed trajectories — fully determined by the trajectory data and policy, both of which depend only on `(seed, methodology flags)`. 

Cell 101's CI/p-values/CE-IS estimates should NOT be used for cross-cell uncertainty comparisons. Only the point estimate is portable.

To strengthen this claim further, a follow-up could re-run cell 101 at full inference reps and verify the point estimate is identical. Not done in this round.

## Vectorized `pearson_residual`

To complete cell 101 in tractable time, `pearson_residual` was vectorized (numpy operations replacing Python list-comp). 16× speedup at small n, ~2× at production scale. **Numerical equivalence verified bit-exact (max abs diff = 0.00e+00) on 1000-row test.**

Subsequently, all 5 list-comp call sites in `dependence.py` were replaced with `pearson_residual_vec`. Tests pass (94/94 in `tests/validate/`). The change applies to ALL future harness runs going forward.

The other 5 cells (010, 001, 011, 101, 111... well, 010/001/011 from Vultr; 101 from Mac with vectorized; 111 from v2 production with un-vectorized) used the un-vectorized pearson_residual. Since vectorized is bit-exact identical, the verdict numbers are comparable. The smoke-test confirmed bit-exact equivalence.

**Methodology change from v2.5**: the percentile CI over 5 fold point estimates (essentially min/max) is replaced by **profile-level paired hierarchical block-bootstrap** on the date axis (Politis–Romano stationary block-bootstrap, expected_block_length=7 days, n_block_bootstrap=500). ### Headline: gate-class transition collapses

**Only cell 000 changes gate-class between methodologies** (BROKEN → REDUCED). All other cells were already REDUCED under v2.5 percentile CI and remain REDUCED under v2.6 block-bootstrap. **Under the adopted block-bootstrap CI method, the v1→v2 gate-class transition that motivated v2.5 no longer obtains.**

The v2.5 BROKEN call for cell 000 was based on `ci_upper = 0.0375` — only 0.7pp below the half-headline threshold (0.04085) on a percentile CI whose grid resolution is 0.83pp (1/120). The classification was effectively sitting on a single-tick separation. Under block-bootstrap, the upper bound widens to 0.1750 — a 4.7× expansion — and the classification flips to REDUCED.

**This artifact claim is threshold-specific.** It pertains to half-headline = 0.04085. For higher thresholds such as 0.08, the v25 percentile CI might place additional cells in BROKEN (cells 010/001/101 with v25_hi = 0.0792 < 0.08) whose v2.6 block-bootstrap CI would also gate REDUCED. The "v1 BROKEN was undercoverage" claim is conditional on the half-headline definition currently in use.

### Point-estimate attribution survives, with precise language

The v2.5 point-estimate attribution for B and C is unchanged by v2.6 — but the precise language matters more now that gate-class is no longer doing rhetorical work.

From cell 000 baseline (0.0083):
- **B alone (cell 010)**: +1.67pp → 0.0250
- **C alone (cell 001)**: +1.67pp → 0.0250
- **B+C combined (cells 011, 111)**: +2.50pp → 0.0333

Single-mode B and C each independently produce a +1.67pp shift; their combined application produces +2.50pp. The +0.83pp synergy comes from one additional successful trajectory in held-out season **2023** (cells 011/111 catch 3/24 in 2023; cells 001/010/101 catch 2/24). **Do not summarize this as "B and C drive +2.5pp" — that hides the saturation pattern.** The accurate framing: "single-mode B and C each shift +1.67pp; combining them produces +2.50pp via one additional 2023 fold success."

### A revisited under v2.6

v2.5 reported "A has zero MARGINAL EFFECT in two measured contrasts (101 vs 001, 111 vs 011)" at scalar resolution. v2.6 strengthens the contrast at fold-level: V001 ≡ V101 and V011 ≡ V111 on point, CI, AND fold-success vector. **A has no observable effect at current resolution** — not just on the coarse scalar but on which held-out seasons succeed and how often.

This is NOT a recommendation to drop A from production methodology. **A (fold-local parameter estimation) is a leakage-hygiene methodology** — it ensures parameter estimation does not use information from the held-out fold. v2.6 measures that A's particular leakage-hygiene impact is below detection on `corrected_pipeline_p57` at n=500, but the property of "training data does not include held-out fold information" remains methodologically defensible regardless.

**Defensible recommendation**: use **pooled-params** (A=off) for **exploratory attribution screens** when compute cost matters. Use **fold-local params** (A=on) for **final defensible audits** where leakage hygiene needs to be demonstrated, accepting the runtime cost.

### Runtime observation (implementation-specific)

In this local n=500 runner, fold-local params (A=on) made the A cells ~3.5× slower (cells 101 and 111 at 17.4–17.5 min) than pooled-params cells (~5.0 min), so A is costly in the current implementation while showing no observable effect. **This is an implementation/runtime cost in this specific Mac n=500 runner, not a universal law** — the per-cell ratio depends on hardware, vectorization, bootstrap structure, and dataset size. Do not generalize the cost ratio without re-measuring.

The cost framing supports the "exploratory vs final-audit" recommendation above: the screening cost gain from running pooled-params is real on this runner but does not warrant dropping A from defensible-audit pipelines.

### Summary: what survives, what collapses, after v2.6

**Survives**:
- Point-estimate attribution: B and C each shift +1.67pp single-mode; +2.50pp combined via 2023 synergy
- "A has no observable effect at current resolution" — strengthened from coarse-scalar to fold-success-pattern level
- Production policy recommendation: keep as-is; pursue distribution-shift remediation

**Collapses**:
- The v1→v2 *gate-class transition* framing — under block-bootstrap CI, no transition occurs at half-headline=0.04085
- Any "drop A" recommendation — A's runtime cost is implementation-specific; A's leakage-hygiene property remains methodologically defensible

**Methodology lesson for future SOTA-audit work**: when a methodology change produces a verdict-gate transition, default to **two robustness checks before announcing**: (1) is the point-estimate attribution actually driven by the change being announced? (v2.5 caught this), and (2) is the gate-class transition robust to alternative CI methods at the same threshold? (v2.6 caught this). Skipping either check leads to overclaim that has to be retracted under review.
